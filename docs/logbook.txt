2026-07-08  -  v1.32 WALK-ABREAST: calm the hold (arrival sprints, then bursts)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Problem (Antonia): v1.3's get-into-position was TOO aggressive as a steady state.
  It re-latched the sprint every time the MOVING anchor drifted past catchUpDist
  (2 m) and only released at inPositionDist (0.8 m) — so while V strolled he was
  sprinting almost constantly, never settling into a calm walk beside her.
- Fix (abreastTick + Config.abreast), Antonia's spec — split arrival from hold:
    1. ARRIVAL: unchanged aggressive sprint (instant heading) until he FIRST closes
       to inPositionDist (0.8 m). New state JL.abreast.arrived latches true there.
       This is now the ONLY unconditional sprint. (catchUpDist is retired.)
    2. HOLD: he holds at Walk and tracks a SLOW averaged gap (EMA over new
       reSprintAvgSeconds = 2 s — Antonia's "2 s slider"). Only when that settled
       gap exceeds reSprintDistFrac (0.30) * radius (~1.05 m) does he fire ONE short
       sprint burst (reSprintBurst = 0.5 s) to reclaim the pocket, then drops back
       to Walk. reSprintCooldown (2 s) stops the laggy average double-firing him.
- arrived/burstUntil/gapAvg all reset when abreast disengages, so every re-engage
  starts with a fresh arrival sprint. All tuned HOLD values (angles, radius,
  smoothSeconds, side hysteresis) unchanged. All 4 new knobs are live in config.
- Version bumped 1.31 -> 1.32.

2026-07-08  -  v1.3 WALK-ABREAST: aggressive get-into-position
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Problem (Antonia): at V's walking pace Jackie could NOT catch up to his beside/
  ahead pocket. He engaged fine, but once he Ran to within catchUpDist (2 m) he
  eased to Walk — then, matching V's walk exactly while the anchor kept drifting
  forward, he could never close the last stretch. Net: he lagged at walk pace.
- Fix (abreastTick + Config.abreast), a 3-phase model matching Antonia's spec:
    1. ENGAGE after V holds the walk band 2 s (walkSustainSeconds 3.0 -> 2.0).
    2. AGGRESSIVE catch-up: catchUpMovement Run -> SPRINT; aim at a near-INSTANT
       heading (new catchUpSmoothSeconds 0.5) so the anchor sits where V is NOW;
       and COMMIT via hysteresis (new inPositionDist 0.8) — he stays in catch-up
       from gap>catchUpDist (2 m) until he's closed to <=0.8 m, so he can't ease
       to a walk at 2 m and stall on a moving anchor.
    3. HOLD: once in the pocket, back to Walk + the slow smoothSeconds (3.3) EMA
       so his movement is "averaged over time" (Antonia's tuned drift, untouched).
- inPos state resets to nil whenever abreast disengages, so every re-engage starts
  in the aggressive sprint-in phase. ALL of Antonia's tuned HOLD values (angles,
  radius, smoothSeconds, side hysteresis) are unchanged — only the catch-up phase
  and the 2 s engage threshold were touched.

2026-07-08  -  v1.3 APPROACH CAMEO (Jackie shows up where V actually is)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
- Problem: his appearance likelihood was too low. He only runs 3-4 of his 7
  venues per day-type, so V rarely bumps into an idle Jackie.
- Fix: new `approachTick` in init.lua + `Config.approach`. When V gets within
  `radius` (20 m) of ANY of his 7 real venues during active hours (06:00-00:00),
  roll ONCE to force his schedule to that venue for the rest of the in-game day.
    * FIRST daily appearance = premiumChance (35%). Rolls stay at 35% on each
      fresh venue-approach UNTIL one lands, then drop to repeatChance (10%) for
      the rest of the day (per Antonia: premium is spent on a SUCCESS, not a
      miss). Noodle bar is ALWAYS noodleChance (10%) — V passes it constantly.
    * Edge-triggered per venue: a venue only re-rolls after V has LEFT its 20 m
      radius and come back, so it can't roll every 2 s tick.
    * The forced venue only overrides the real schedule while V is within
      proximityRadius (45 m) of it, so an early force never suppresses his
      genuinely-scheduled spot when V is elsewhere. Resets each in-game day.
    * Sleep window (00:00-06:00) is untouched — left to the secret-nap cameo.
- Note: `approachTick` is a GLOBAL function (not `local`) — init.lua is at Lua's
  200-locals-per-chunk cap, so a new top-level `local` would break the load.
  Its constants live in Config; its internals are function-scoped locals (fine).

- Antonia re-tested the v0.8 recorder: "Recorder: ON" but NOTHING recorded, even

  AudioSystem:Play can't see native barks. Dead end (matches "0 events unfiltered").
- FIX (v0.10): hook the SCRIPTED subtitle controller instead. The game calls
  SubtitlesGameController:OnHideLine(lineData) for every displayed line, so CET can
  observe it (proven by shipping subtitle mods, e.g. "Subtitles - Two Lines").
  v0.10 reads lineData.text / .speakerName / .id (the String ID), filters to Jackie
  by speaker name/record (works for ANY Jackie, not just our spawned entity),
  dedupes by String ID, and a "Dump to file" button writes recorded_lines.json into
  the mod folder (CET io is sandboxed there). REQUIRES subtitles ON. Deployed.

     subtitle hook didn't fire either. We have never confirmed CET Observe fires at
     all in this install. Need the "Recorder hook registered: ..." console line to
     tell registration-failure (wrong class for v2.3) from firing-failure (subs off
     / wrong method). DE-PRIORITISED: the scraper catalogue already identifies lines.
